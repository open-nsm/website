This is a user driven group, if you would like to present on a topic please send an e-mail to the coordination mailing list.

The Open (-Source) Network Security Monitoring Group (OpenNSM) meets at 5:30pm on Mondays.
We will meet in room 301 (3rd floor) of the CSL (Coordinated Science Lab) building
The GoToMeeting invitation is sent to the mailing list in the meeting notes and in the Facebook event.

We're not aware of any other active NSM user groups in the United States, and have the ambitious goal of being a premier place for students, professionals, and hobbyists, from all over to share their research, tools, and techniques in a laid back and friendly environment.

Double entendre - Open as in public and as in Open-Source since most tools we will be playing with will be FOSS

OpenNSM aims to provide a place for network security analysts and those interested in information security to share tricks, solutions, work on projects, and other knowledge about the subject. Come join us!

Areas of focus:

  1. The Bro Programming Language
  2. Libpcap programming
  3. Writing IDS Signatures
  4. Packet Analysis
  5. Log Analysis
  6. Malware Analysis
  7. IDS and Firewall Evasion Techniques
  8. SecurityOnion Enhancements
  9. Much more!

Note: Doors close at 6 PM so if you're late someone will have to let you in or attend remotely.

(We also run the Linux User Group so come join us at LUG in the Siebel Center after.)
